01.2
The diagnosis · LinkedIn

What works, where engagement leaks

EPA is doing the work. The foundation is there. The gap is in how that work gets packaged for the feed.

What's already working

Keep
Owns the right conversation. AI, APDS, kerbside management, EU affairs, traffic and parking integration. The topics map cleanly to where the sector is moving.
Content pillars are established. Event announcements, news updates, video coverage. The structure is there - it just needs sharper templates and a clearer system.
Proves real activity. Events, working groups, publications, partnerships, member initiatives. The page shows EPA actually does what it claims.
Top performers are videos. The highest-reaction posts in the sample are video-led (66, 67, 57 reactions). Motion and people consistently sit at the top of the distribution.
Events become multi-post moments. Intertraffic Amsterdam was turned into a sustained series across announcements, sessions, recaps and reposts. The right instinct - just needs sharper templates.
Tone is credible. Collaborative, appropriate for a European association. The voice is not the issue.

Where engagement leaks

Fix
Cadence is bursty, not paced. Three posts in one week, then silence for three weeks. The content doesn't lose relevance, but the algorithm needs to be fed every week. A steady weekly post outperforms uneven bursts.
Too many links in the body. LinkedIn deprioritises posts that send traffic off-platform. One link in-post is fine. Three or four becomes a drag on reach. Those belong in the first comment.
Templates don't scale. The speaker-announcement template works at 7 speakers but breaks with 2 - visible empty diagonal space where speakers should be.
Tone wobbles in video. Some clips are playful and energetic, others are calm and reflective. No rule for which register fits which moment.
Brand drift across assets. Colours and typography wobble - visible effort, but no enforced system. The account isn't yet instantly recognisable in the feed.
CTAs send people off-platform. "Check out this report", "fill out this form". LinkedIn doesn't reward this - the algorithm wants people staying and commenting, not clicking away.
The pattern

The foundation is there. Here's how to improve the packaging.

Healthy cadence, right topics, right audience, visible production effort. The work in front of EPA is in pacing, format mix, template flexibility, brand consistency, and how posts ask for engagement.

01.3
The fix · LinkedIn

Three workstreams, not one

Splitting the work into three concrete tracks, each with its own deliverable.

1System

Rebuild the templates

A flexible visual system that flexes with content. One-time investment, infinite reuse.

  • Speaker template with 1, 2, 3, 4+ variants - no empty space
  • Branded video lower-thirds and end cards
  • Typography rules enforced across every asset
  • Your recurring formats
2Voice

Define the messaging house

The single document that governs everything EPA says on LinkedIn - and outside it.

  • The value each post type delivers to its reader
  • Vocabulary EPA uses, and vocabulary EPA avoids
  • Key messages by pillar - policy, future of parking, community, knowledge, partners
  • Hook formula: insight before activity, sector implication before logistics
  • One message, one audience, one CTA per post
3Packaging

Repackage the posts

Apply the system. Pace the cadence. Shift CTAs from outbound to inbound.

  • Weekly steady cadence - kill bursts and silences
  • Cap body-text links: one in-post, the rest go to comments
  • Shift to discussion-driven CTAs (examples below)
  • Light QC pass on every post: tags, hashtags, alt text, names

02.1
The diagnosis · Website

A database that wants to be a portal

The website holds enormous content depth and institutional credibility. The barrier isn't substance - it's structure, hierarchy, and how that depth is presented.

What's already working

Keep
Looks legitimate and European-level. The institutional positioning lands immediately. There's no credibility gap to close.
Deep content volume. Working groups, parking data initiatives, APDS, European Parking Card, conferences, ESG themes. The depth is real.
Strong topical authority. Industry-specific terminology, structured thematic areas, real sector expertise visible across pages.
Mobile basics hold. No major bugs, the site behaves on phones - even if scannability needs work.

Where it leaks

Fix
Menu overload. Too many first-level items, repeated terminology, hard to scan. The navigation is the single biggest barrier on the site.
Homepage is a feed, not a landing page. Reads like a content river with an association description on top. No focused CTAs, no audience routing, weak visual hierarchy.
No journey for different audiences. Operators, municipalities, policymakers, media and members all land on the same homepage. None is guided to what they need.
Visual design feels template-driven. Dead space on the sides, dated typography, no differentiation between pages, no clear H1/H2/H3 hierarchy - content reads as undifferentiated plain text.
Imagery is generic stock. Real photos are low-resolution. There's no mobility-tech visual language - no smart city, EV charging, or urban mobility imagery anchoring the mission.
News, events, publications presented as flat lists. No card layouts, no filters, no search. Users have to scroll through everything to find what they need.
No engagement funnels. No clear membership funnel, no newsletter strategy, no conference funnel, no Working Groups CTA. The site doesn't ask the visitor to do anything.
Compliance gaps. No visible cookie consent banner during testing. No privacy policy despite forms collecting user data. Configuration should be reviewed.
SEO depth is wasted. Strong content volume and structured thematic areas, but pages feel structurally similar to crawlers, and internal linking opportunities go unused.
02.2
The fix · Website

Three workstreams for the website

Same logic as the LinkedIn fix: split a fuzzy redesign brief into three concrete tracks.

1Architecture

Simplify the navigation

Cut the menu down to what matters. Move depth to where depth belongs.

  • 6 main menu items: About / Initiatives / Insights / Events & Awards / Membership / Contact
  • Sub-topics (Working Groups, ESG, APDS, Parking Data) become structured landing pages
  • Mega menu pattern for first-level navigation
  • Full menu in the footer - not the header
2Hierarchy

Rebuild the homepage

From content feed to strategic landing page. Guide visitors, don't drown them.

  • Audience routing: cards for operators, cities, policymakers, members, media
  • Key focus areas surfaced visually, not buried in text
  • Clear visual differentiation: news vs events vs magazine vs newsletter
  • Featured item or highlighted news above the fold
  • Focused CTAs: Become a Member · Join an EPA Event · Subscribe to Updates
3Polish

Modernise and tighten

Visual language, compliance, content presentation. The polish that makes depth feel premium.

  • Mobility-tech imagery: smart city, EV charging, urban mobility, conferences (real photos in HQ)
  • Card systems for news, publications, events - with search and filters
  • Typography hierarchy: clear H1, H2, H3, body, callouts
  • Cookie banner, privacy policy, GDPR review
  • Internal linking strategy to unlock SEO depth
The shift

From documenting activity to packaging authority.
